# Further reading: a proposed biological mechanism (not part of this study)

This folder is **background reference only**. It is **not part of our analysis, data, or results**, and we did not produce the materials in it.

Our paper reports an **association**: among U.S. adolescents, bullying victimization is linked specifically to non-medical prescription opioid use. In the Discussion we mention one **possible biological reason** this association might exist -- the idea that social pain and physical pain share the brain's opioid system, so opioids may relieve social pain in particular (the "social-pain-to-opioid" idea).

The peer-reviewed review below lays out that mechanism in detail. It is included here for readers who want to understand the proposed biology, or who are skeptical of it and want to read the primary source. **This mechanism is a hypothesis for *why* our association may occur; our study does not test it.**
